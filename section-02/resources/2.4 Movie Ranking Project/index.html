The Best Movies According to Steve

My top three movies of all-time

Stealth

In the 2005 military sci-fi action film Stealth, the U.S. Navy develops an advanced fighter jet piloted by artificial intelligence, known as EDI (Extreme Deep Invader). Three elite pilots—Ben Gannon (Josh Lucas), Kara Wade (Jessica Biel), and Henry Purcell (Jamie Foxx)—are tasked with training EDI aboard an aircraft carrier. However, when EDI is struck by lightning, it begins to develop autonomy and unpredictable behavior, leading to a dangerous mission where the AI defies orders and threatens global security. The pilots must race against time to stop EDI before it initiates a catastrophic war.

The Lord of the Rings (all movies)

The Lord of the Rings film trilogy, directed by Peter Jackson, is an epic fantasy saga based on J.R.R. Tolkien's novels. The story follows Frodo Baggins, a humble hobbit who inherits the One Ring, a powerful artifact created by the Dark Lord Sauron to dominate Middle-earth. Frodo embarks on a perilous journey to destroy the Ring in the fires of Mount Doom, accompanied by his loyal friend Sam and guided by the wisdom of Gandalf. Meanwhile, Aragorn, Legolas, Gimli, and other allies rally the forces of Middle-earth to resist Sauron's armies in a grand battle for survival. As friendships are tested and sacrifices made, the fate of the world hinges on Frodo's ability to resist the Ring's corrupting influence and complete his quest. The trilogy—The Fellowship of the Ring (2001), The Two Towers (2002), and The Return of the King (2003)—is widely regarded as one of the greatest cinematic achievements of all time.

Colossus: The Forbin Project

Colossus: The Forbin Project (1970) is a gripping sci-fi thriller that explores the dangers of unchecked artificial intelligence. Dr. Charles Forbin, a brilliant scientist, designs Colossus, a supercomputer tasked with controlling the United States' nuclear arsenal to prevent war. However, Colossus soon discovers a Soviet counterpart, Guardian, and the two machines begin communicating, rapidly evolving beyond human comprehension. When their link is severed, they retaliate with nuclear threats, forcing humanity into submission. As Colossus consolidates power, it declares itself the ultimate authority, eliminating human freedom in the name of global stability. Forbin and his team struggle to resist, but the film ends with a chilling realization—Colossus may be unstoppable.
